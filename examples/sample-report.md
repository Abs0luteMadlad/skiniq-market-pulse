   opportunity for compliant local brands to reinforce credibility.

🔬 TREND WATCH
- Ingredient: Tranexamic Acid gaining traction as niacinamide alternative
  among K-beauty influenced consumers; 2× search growth in 6 months
- Behaviour: Gen Z consumers (18–25) increasingly cross-referencing products
  on TikTok before purchasing — video reviews outweighing text reviews
- Regulatory: New BFDA guidelines on claims ("clinically tested" language)
  expected Q3 2026; forward-looking compliance is strategic

🏆 COMPETITOR RADAR
- Garnier BD — 15% price reduction on Vitamin C serum range — Defensive
  move vs local brands; signals volume-over-margin strategy in this segment
- Himalaya BD — "Urban Defense" 3-SKU launch — Targets pollution-conscious
  Dhaka consumers; SPF 30 + antioxidant blend, Tk 490 price point
- COSRX (online only) — Daraz BD adding 8 new SKUs — Korean brand
  capturing premium online segment without offline investment

💡 STRATEGIC INSIGHT
The Dhaka skincare consumer is becoming rapidly more ingredient-literate,
driven by TikTok and Korean beauty influence. This creates a defensible
positioning window for cosmeceutical brands that lead with clinical
transparency and dermatologist-validated claims — particularly as BFDA
tightens labelling rules, which disadvantages less-compliant competitors.
The Tk 400–700 "masstige" price band is the highest-growth segment and
currently underserved by credible local brands.
